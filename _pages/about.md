I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at Emory University, specializing in political economy, conflict and violence, and quantitative methods. My research examines how political and economic incentives shape violence, governance, and political behavior, with a particular focus on developing and post-conflict contexts.


My work combines formal theory, causal inference, and large-scale observational data, drawing on administrative records, original data collection, and remote sensing. Substantively, I study political violence, civilian victimization, electoral incentives, disaster relief, and the political consequences of resource flows and external actors. My dissertation brings together these themes to examine how institutions and incentives shape both violence and accountability.

At William Jewell, I also played collegiate soccer.

That experience continues to inform my interest in applied research and policy-relevant questions.
